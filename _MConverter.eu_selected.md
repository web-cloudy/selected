**Abstract**

Bitcoin exhibits a highly distinctive four-year price cycle
characterized by strong expansions followed by deep corrections. While
early analysts believed these cycles were accidental coincidences,
deeper structural economic analysis reveals the cycles originate from
Bitcoin's programmed supply issuance (the halving), miner economics,
liquidity cycles, and reflexive market psychology. This paper provides a
deeply detailed explanation of why these cycles exist, what drives

future Bitcoin prices can be forecasted using multi-model analysis.

**1. Introduction**

Bitcoin is unique because it is the first asset with a *perfectly
predictable, non-negotiable monetary policy*. Unlike gold (slow
unpredictable supply), fiat currencies (inflation varies), or stocks
(supply changes through buybacks or issuance), Bitcoin's supply issuance
is algorithmic and reduces every four years.

This deterministic design creates a repeating economic shock called the
**halving**, which historically produces a predictable four-phase market
cycle:

1.  Accumulation

2.  Expansion

3.  Parabolic (Euphoria)

4.  Correction (Bear)

These cycles have repeated *almost perfectly* over 12+ years.  
Understanding *why* this happens requires examining:

- miner revenue mechanisms

- supply shocks

- liquidity dynamics

- investor psychology

- on-chain economic behavior

This paper dives deeply into each mechanism.

**2. Why Bitcoin\'s 4-Year Cycle Happens (In Deep Detail)**

Here we break down the complex mechanisms so they're easy to understand,
even if the underlying economics are advanced.

**2.1 Bitcoin Halving and Supply Shock**

Every \~4 years, Bitcoin's block reward halves:

| **Year** | **Block Reward Before** | **Reward After** |
|----------|-------------------------|------------------|
| 2012     | 50 →                    | 25               |
| 2016     | 25 →                    | 12.5             |
| 2020     | 12.5 →                  | 6.25             |
| 2024     | 6\. 25 →                | 3.125            |

**This means Bitcoin inflation rate drops by 50% instantly every 4
years.**

**Why is this important?**

Supply and demand theory states:

When supply drops sharply and demand stays the same (or increases), the
price must rise.

But the halving supply shock is not small --- it\'s massive.

Before the 2024 halving:

- \~900 BTC produced per day  
  After halving:

- \~450 BTC per day

Even at \$50,000/BTC, that's a supply reduction of *\$22.5 million per
day*.  
Institutional demand (ETFs) often exceeds \$50--200 million/day.

Thus **halving creates a large imbalance in supply vs demand**, which is
the core reason for the cycle.

**2.2 Miner Economics (Most People Don't Understand This Part)**

Bitcoin miners are not just confirming transactions --- they are the
**forced sellers** of new Bitcoin.

How it works:

- Miners earn BTC rewards + fees.

- To pay for electricity and hardware, they must **sell part of their
  BTC daily**.

- The halving suddenly cuts their revenue 50%.

This creates a shock:

- Some miners become unprofitable → shut down → sell their reserves.

- Surviving miners must operate more efficiently → buy better hardware →
  need capital → more selling.

This entire process creates **strong volatility** around halving
periods.

**2.3 Reflexive Market Psychology (George Soros' Reflexivity)**

This is extremely important:

**Investors expect the halving to make the price go up.**  
This expectation causes early accumulation → early price rise → triggers
media attention → triggers more buying → triggers mania.

This positive feedback loop is called **reflexivity**.

It explains why:

- Bitcoin rises faster than fundamentals justify during bull markets.

- Bitcoin crashes harder during bear markets than fundamentals justify.

**2.4 Liquidity Cycles Align With Halvings**

This is a complex but critical concept.

**Key principle:**

Bitcoin price strongly correlates with **global liquidity (M2 money
supply)**.

When central banks:

- Lower interest rates → increase liquidity → risk assets pump

- Raise interest rates → liquidity tightens → Bitcoin dumps

It turns out many halving cycles **coincidentally line up with global
liquidity expansions**:

| **Cycle** | **Halving Year**        | **Liquidity Trend** | **Result** |
|-----------|-------------------------|---------------------|------------|
| 2012      | QE3 (massive)           | Huge bull           |            |
| 2016      | Low rates               | Strong bull         |            |
| 2020      | COVID QE (largest ever) | Mega bull           |            |
| 2024      | Uptrend starting        | Institutional bull  |            |

So halvings + liquidity = perfect storm for bubbles.

After the 2024 halving:

- hash rate reached new highs (more competition)

Many miners were forced to:

- sell BTC

- power off machines

- enter "capitulation zones"

Miner capitulation historically marks cycle bottoms.

**4.3 ETF Inflow Slowdown**

In early 2024:

But when inflows slow down or turn negative, demand collapses.

**4.4 Leverage Unwinding**

When price corrects:

- long positions get liquidated

- this creates forced selling

- which triggers more liquidations

A cascade effect.

This often exaggerates corrections.
