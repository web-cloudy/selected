**Bitcoin’s cycle.**
The Bitcoin halving cycle refers to the recurring ~4-year pattern in Bitcoin's price behavior, closely tied to the Bitcoin halving event.
This is a programmed mechanism in Bitcoin's protocol where the block reward (new BTC issued to miners for validating transactions) is cut in half approximately every four years, or every 210,000 blocks.
The goal is to control Bitcoin's inflation and enforce its fixed supply cap of 21 million coins.
Key Historical Halvings
First: November 28, 2012 (reward from 50 BTC → 25 BTC)
Second: July 9, 2016 (25 BTC → 12.5 BTC)
Third: May 11, 2020 (12.5 BTC → 6.25 BTC)
Fourth: April 19/20, 2024 (6.25 BTC → 3.125 BTC)

The next one is expected around April 2028 (to 1.5625 BTC).
The Traditional Halving Cycle Theory in crypto says that halvings reduce the rate of new Bitcoin supply entering the market, creating scarcity.
If demand stays steady or grows (from adoption, speculation, institutional interest, etc.), this often leads to significant price appreciation.
Historically, patterns include: A pre-halving rally (build-up anticipation).
Post-halving consolidation or dip (sometimes months of sideways/lows).
Then a major bull run peaking roughly 12–18 months after the halving.
Followed by a bear market/crash (often 70–80%+ drawdown), accumulation phase, and eventual recovery into the next cycle.

Examples: After 2012: BTC surged from ~$12 to over $1,000 by late 2013.
After 2016: From ~$650 to nearly $20,000 by late 2017.
After 2020: From ~$8,500–$9,000 to an all-time high (ATH) of ~$69,000 in late 2021.

This creates the famous 4-year cycle: halving → bull → peak → bear → accumulation → next halving.
Current Situation (as of February 12, 2026), Bitcoin's price today is hovering around $66,000–$67,000.
The 2024 halving occurred about 22 months ago.
In past cycles, this timeframe often aligned with or approached the major bull phase/peak.
However, Bitcoin did rally post-2024 halving, reaching a new ATH around $126,000 in October 2025 (roughly 18 months post-halving, fitting the historical timing for peaks).
Since then, it has corrected significantly—down about 45–50% from that peak, trading in the mid-$60,000s now, with some reports of brief dips below $61,000 earlier in 2026.
This represents a notable drawdown, and some analysts describe it as potentially the "halfway point" of a bear phase or a deeper correction than in prior cycles.
So, is it following the Traditional Theory?
Partially yes, but with deviations suggesting the cycle may be evolving or "breaking" from the exact historical template.
Yes elements: The post-2024 halving bull run materialized, with a peak ~18 months later (similar to prior cycles). Scarcity from the halving, plus factors like spot ETFs, institutional adoption, and macro events, drove the rally to new highs.
No/deviations: The peak arrived, but the subsequent correction has been sharp and ongoing into early 2026, without a clear new bull resumption yet.
Some observers note faster momentum fade, possibly due to matured market (more institutional involvement, less retail euphoria), macro pressures (e.g., stock market slumps, Fed uncertainties), or an "extended cycle" narrative.
Traditional cycles often saw peaks followed by multi-year bears, but this drawdown feels pronounced mid-cycle.

Overall, Bitcoin still shows cyclical behavior tied to halvings.
But each cycle has been less explosive in percentage gains (diminishing returns as the market matures). The theory holds as a framework, but external factors increasingly influence outcomes.
We're likely in a post-peak consolidation/bear phase, with the next halving in 2028 potentially resetting for future growth.
